GOVERNANCE GLOSSARY

Purpose:

Define common governance terminology to ensure consistent interpretation
of governance documentation.

────────────────────────────────

Authority

Definition:
The entity permitted to make decisions.

In this system:
Authority remains human-directed.

────────────────────────────────

Governance

Definition:
Structural definition of expectations without runtime authority.

────────────────────────────────

Enforcement

Definition:
Translation of governance expectations into actionable rules.

Status:
Not implemented.

────────────────────────────────

Execution

Definition:
Runtime behavior of systems and workers.

Status:
Governance is not coupled to execution.

────────────────────────────────

Eligibility

Definition:
Conditions required before an action may occur.

────────────────────────────────

Containment

Definition:
Limiting the impact radius of failure.

────────────────────────────────

Isolation

Definition:
Preventing unintended cross-system interaction.

────────────────────────────────

Reliability

Definition:
Consistency of expected behavior over time.

────────────────────────────────

Determinism

Definition:
Same inputs produce same outputs.

────────────────────────────────

Traceability

Definition:
Ability to follow decisions and changes through history.

────────────────────────────────

Accountability

Definition:
Clear ownership of decisions and outcomes.

────────────────────────────────

Observability

Definition:
Ability to understand system behavior through signals.

────────────────────────────────

Governance Boundary

Definition:
Explicit limits of governance authority.

Governance does not control execution.

────────────────────────────────

Governance Posture

Definition:

Documentation-first  
Human-authority-preserving  
Execution-isolated  
Non-autonomous  

────────────────────────────────

NOTE

This glossary defines terminology only.

It introduces no authority.
It introduces no enforcement capability.
It introduces no execution behavior.
