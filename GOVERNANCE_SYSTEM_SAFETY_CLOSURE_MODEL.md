# GOVERNANCE SYSTEM SAFETY CLOSURE MODEL
## Phase 269

---

## PURPOSE

Define how governance cognition reaches safety completeness
without introducing enforcement behavior.

Safety closure establishes:

Governance completeness visibility
Remaining governance exposure
Safety readiness boundaries
Cognition completion indicators

Closure prepares safety awareness only.

Closure does NOT introduce execution authority.

---

## SAFETY CLOSURE PRINCIPLE

Governance safety closure must always be:

Deterministic
Explainable
Traceable
Operator visible

Closure must never be:

Implicit
Automatically enforced
Self-declared complete

Safety closure must always remain reviewable.

---

## GOVERNANCE SAFETY COMPLETENESS

Safety completeness must evaluate:

Doctrine coverage
Constraint lifecycle coverage
Validation coverage
Risk classification coverage
Operator review coverage
Authority preservation guarantees
Governance boundary protection

Completeness must produce:

Coverage state only.

---

## REMAINING GOVERNANCE EXPOSURE

Closure must identify:

Unvalidated constraints
Incomplete lifecycle states
Unclassified risks
Unreviewed governance elements
Doctrine alignment gaps
Governance drift exposure

Exposure identification must remain:

Read-only.

## SAFETY MODEL CLOSURE CONDITIONS

Governance cognition may be considered structurally complete when:

All constraints registered
All constraints lifecycle tracked
All governance layers defined
Doctrine integration complete
Authority preservation verified
Governance boundaries defined

Closure must never imply:

System autonomy.

End of governance cognition preparation

Human authority

Governance non-escalation

Authorize enforcement automatically.
